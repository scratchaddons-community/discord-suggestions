I work for a non-profit that teaches coding to elementary school teachers/students nationally. Many MANY educators, School Districts and even State Departments of Education across the US won't allow their students to use the online version Scratch in school because of perceived dangers/distractions caused by the search bar and the comments section. While having these is an integral to component to learning coding, so is the ability to USE the platform by getting it unbanned in many schools. Teachers have the obligation to protect their students, and both the search bar and comments section cause issues for them in their duty. A toggle that simply hides these would open countless doors for young students to learn coding, and for districts to utilize Scratch and ScratchAddons.
