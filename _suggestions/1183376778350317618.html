A new way to edit your vector shapes.
A mode for __Slicing__, which can have up to four distinct modes, being ...
A: Separate. In case you want to keep most of the information, but split it up.
B: Delete Above.
C: Delete Below. (These two really speak for themselves).
D: Cut-Out.
(Note: I'm not suggesting all modes be added, purely suggestions to flesh out concept)
The general usefulness comes with the ability to Separate or Remove unwanted information quickly.
Examples of use? You can use __Slice Mode D__ to cut out one frame in a large portrait of vector images, to have a perfect square of information without needing to mindlessly wittle it down manually (probably for an hour or so). It can be frustrating dealing with how the shape distorts when you add or remove vector nodes on the edges when you don't want the "general shape" to change at all.
With __Slice Mode A__ you could easily take chunks off existing sprites and move those parts elsewhere. Let's say, you have a Barn. You want to show that it's falling apart. You can now easily take chunks off the sprite and lay them out as you see fit, then making any other aesthetic changes you need to complete the look.
Slice Modes B & C become pretty clear by this point but instead of keeping most of the information you are cutting, this would allow you to get rid of something quickly. If you wanted to really get deep, then you could consider Left & Right as well, but I'm getting ahead of myself.
=~=~= To be clear, I'm not making multiple suggestions, I'm just laying out how this feature could be utilized in different ways, but with the core premise of being able to "Slice" through your vector objects. If there are too many "modes" listed, then consider "Separate" and "Cut-Out" as the most useful, though all have their merits. Thanks for your consideration. =~=~=
